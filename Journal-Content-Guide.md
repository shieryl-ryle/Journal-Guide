# ✍️ Journal Entry Guide  

This is the guide and template for writing your **weekly journal entries**.  
Your journal will help you reflect, track your progress, and show your learning journey in Data Engineering. 🚀  

---

## 📑 Journal Entry Template  

Here’s the structure you should follow.  
Copy this format and replace it with your own answers.  

```markdown
# Journal — YYYY-MM-DD — Day X: [Short Title]

## 1) What I learned 
- Bullet points of what you learned today.

## 2) New vocabulary (define in your own words)
- **Term** – your own simple explanation.

## 3) Data Engineering mindset applied (what principles did I use?)
- How you applied DE principles or best practices.

## 4) Decisions & assumptions (why, alternatives, trade-offs)
- Choices you made and why. Mistakes are fine too!

## 5) Open questions (things I still don’t get)
- Write down at least 1 question you’re curious about.

## 6) Next actions (small, doable steps)
- [ ] Example: review Git basics
- [ ] Example: practice writing SQL queries

## 7) Artifacts & links (code, queries, dashboards)
- Links to repos, dashboards, or screenshots.

---

### Mini reflection (3–5 sentences)
What surprised you? What would you do differently?  
What will you watch out for in the future?

---

### BONUS: Meme of the day
**Caption:** [funny or relatable caption here]  

![meme](../assets/meme-sample.jpg "describe your meme")
```

## 🖼️ Adding Images to Your Journal  

1. Create a folder named **`assets`** inside your repository.  
   - Example: `/assets/meme-docker.jpg`  
2. Place your image inside that folder.  
3. In your journal entry, link the image like this:  

```markdown
![alt text](../assets/meme-docker.jpg "optional title")
```

- `alt text` → shows when image doesn’t load (also for accessibility).  
- `"optional title"` → shows when you hover over the image.  

✅ Example in a journal:  

```markdown
### BONUS: Meme of the day
**Caption:** my face while fixing my Docker while sir Myk is getting stressed  

![meme](../assets/meme-docker.jpg "my face while fixing Docker")
```

---

## 📌 Tips for Writing Journals  

- Be **honest** — it’s for your learning journey.  
- Be **consistent** — small entries daily are better than long ones once a week.  
- Use **bullet points** to keep it simple.  
- Add **fun elements** (memes, screenshots, doodles) to make it enjoyable.  

---

✅ Now you’re ready! Create a new file for each entry:  

- Example: `journals/yourname/2025-09-13.md`  
- Example: `journals/yourname/2025-09-14.md`  

And follow the template each day 🎉
